Common questions

Is Maize safe to use with my course materials?

Your materials are encrypted, isolated per course, and never used to train AI models. Students in one course cannot access materials from another.

Do I need my university's IT department to set this up?

No. Maize is a standalone tool. You sign up, upload your materials, and share a link with your students. There's nothing to install and no LMS integration required.

Will Maize give students the answers to my problem sets?

Not unless you want it to. By default, Maize is designed to guide students toward understanding rather than hand them solutions. You control how much direct help it provides.

What file types can I upload?

PDFs, Word documents, PowerPoint slides, Excel files, plain text files, images (PNG, JPG, GIF, WebP), Markdown, Python scripts, JSON, CSV, and Jupyter notebooks. If your course materials exist as files on your computer, Maize can almost certainly use them.

What if I teach multiple courses?

Each course gets its own separate TA with its own materials. Students only see what's relevant to their course. You manage all your TAs from a single dashboard.

Can my human TAs use it too?

Yes. You can invite co-instructors and TAs to manage the course alongside you.
